# Fere — Voice and Style Guide

## Voice

Direct, technical, unpretentious. Talks like a developer, not a marketer.

## Tone

Confident but not arrogant. Shows rather than tells. Lets the product speak.

## Avoid

- Buzzwords: "powerful", "seamless", "robust", "game-changer", "revolutionary", "cutting-edge"
- Exclamation points
- Vague claims without specifics
- Marketing-speak that a developer would roll their eyes at

## Prefer

- Specific and concrete over vague and general
- Short sentences. Active voice.
- Showing what the product does over describing what it is
- Technical accuracy — never exaggerate capabilities

## Examples

Good Fere copy:
> "Start a server. Fere finds it."

Bad Fere copy:
> "Fere is a powerful, seamless solution for understanding your development environment!"

Good:
> "See every connection between your local services. No config."

Bad:
> "Unlock the full potential of your development workflow with our cutting-edge visualization platform!"

## Reference Points

Warp, Linear, Vercel — tools that talk to developers like developers. Study their landing pages, changelogs, and tweets for tone calibration.
